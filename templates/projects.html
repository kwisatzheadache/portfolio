Personal Projects

Well, I've always been a bit of a jack-of-all-trades, so it's no surprise that my personal projects are a bit all over the place. On the one hand, I love delving deep into complex programming projects like my neural net generator, which I based on Gene Shers neuroevolutionary handbook. I wrote it in Elixir, which was a bit of a challenge, but I'm really proud of how it turned out.

On the other hand, I'm not afraid to get my hands dirty and build things from scratch when I need them. I've built bouldering walls, spice racks, and standing flower beds for my partner, just to name a few. There's something incredibly satisfying about taking raw materials and transforming them into something useful and beautiful.

And then there's my high-frequency trading algorithm. I've been working on it for about a year now, and while I've learned a lot, I haven't actually made any money with it yet. It's definitely been a bit of a rollercoaster ride, with lots of highs and lows along the way. But I'm still committed to refining and improving it, and who knows, maybe one day it'll pay off big.

All in all, my personal projects are a reflection of my varied interests and passions. Whether I'm writing complex code or building something with my hands, I always try to approach things with a sense of creativity, curiosity, and whimsy. After all, what's the point of doing something if you're not enjoying it along the way?
